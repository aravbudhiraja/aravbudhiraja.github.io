HISTORY OF THESSALONIKI

EARLY BYZANTINE CHURCHES

The Church of Panagia Acheiropoietos

The Church of Panagia Acheiropoietos stands as a remarkable testament to early Christian architecture and the city's rich religious heritage. Constructed in the mid-5th century, around 450–470 AD, it is among the oldest surviving churches in Thessaloniki.

The Church of Agia Sophia

Constructed in the 7th century AD, it replaced an earlier basilica dedicated to Saint Mark, which had been destroyed at the beginning of the century. Over the centuries, it served as a cathedral during the Crusader occupation in 1205, a mosque under Ottoman rule after 1430, and finally being restored to its original function as a Christian church following Thessaloniki’s liberation in 1912.

learn more

The Church of Hosios David

The Church of Hosios David, formerly the katholikon of the Latomos Monastery, is among the most important monuments of early Christian and Byzantine art in Thessaloniki. It was built at the end of the 5th century and is famous for its exquisite mosaics and frescoes, a real treasure of the city's religious heritage.

learn more

The Church of Agios Dimitrios

The Church of Agios Dimitrios in Thessaloniki is a historic and spiritual landmark dedicated to Saint Demetrius, the city’s patron saint. Renowned for its Byzantine architecture and mosaics, it has endured centuries of change, remaining a central symbol of faith and heritage.
